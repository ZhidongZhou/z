This dialogue takes place at a basketball club between a mandarin speaking parent of a teenage boy and an Australian senior sports coach, Mr. Harris.”

ID Original Translated
E1 Good morning Mr. Lin. You sounded quite worried over the phone. How can I help you? 16
M1 哈里斯先生，感谢您能提早赶来见我。我想让我儿子转到另外一个球队，所以想听听您的意见。 Mr. Harris, thank you for coming early to see me. I’d like to have my son transferred to another team, so I’m hoping you can give me some advice.
E2 Well, generally I wouldn’t recommend moving kids around in the middle of the season, but can you tell me why you think he needs to change to another team? 29
M2 我觉得他呆在 B 队并不开心。我问过他很多次，但他总说没什么。我觉得可能队友欺负他，或歧视他。 I don’t think he is happy staying in Team B. I’ve asked him many times but he always says there’s nothing to worry about. I think his teammates may be bullying him or discriminating against him.
E3 If it is true that he is being bullied or discriminated against as you suspect, this is a very serious matter. There are certain procedures we should follow to make sure such behaviour is stopped immediately. 36
M3 感谢您能严肃对待此事。我怀疑的依据是我儿子训练回家后所说的一些小事，比如“为什么我不像其他人一样出生在澳洲”或者“他们总排斥我”。 Thank you for taking this matter seriously. My suspicion stems from some little things my son mentioned when he came home from training, like “why was I not born in Australia like everybody else” or “they always exclude me”.
E4 We’ll first need to find out if your suspicion is justified. Could you have a talk with your son to find out who said those things? Also, get him to tell you some examples of incidents that have upset him. 40
M4 我试试看吧，但我觉得他不一定会愿意说。我并不很擅长与人沟通，所以我对是否能做到你让我做的没有信心。 I’ll give it a shot but I don’t think he is willing to tell me. I’m not good at communicating with people so I don’t have confidence in doing what you’ve asked me to do.
E5 If that’s the case, you might want to have your son sit down with Chris Paul who is our club’s child protection officer. He also happens to be a practising counsellor. Your son should feel comfortable talking with Chris. 39
M5 这太好了。我儿子在家的时候，总是少言寡语的，但我觉得他可能会更愿意跟辅导员聊聊心事。那么，如果他真的是被欺负或受到歧视了呢？ That’s great. My son doesn’t talk much at home, and I think he may be more willing to talk to a counsellor. What if he really has been bullied or discriminated against?
E6 If we find out that your son has indeed been bullied or discriminated against, we will follow the government’s guidelines for solving such problems in sporting and recreation clubs. 29
M6 我希望我儿子能够像他最近迷上的 NBA 球员林书豪一样，克服这些困难，开开心心地参加自己热爱的运动。我今天回去先和他谈谈，然后我们下周见！ I hope my son can overcome these difficulties and enjoy the sport he likes, like Jeremy Lin, his new NBA idol. I’ll talk with him when I get back and I’ll see you next week.
