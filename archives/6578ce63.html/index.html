租赁纠纷

相遇
19-07-2020
Hits
学习笔记 › NATTI › CCL
CCL 

        

        




This is a dialogue between Mr. Chen (C), a Mandarin speaking man, and Steven (S), a solicitor in the local community legal centre. Mr. Chen is seeking advice on problems between him and his landlord.








ID
Original
Translated





M1
你好。我想问一下 Steven 先生在不在？我跟他约了今天十点钟的会面。 Hello, I’d like to know whether Steven is here or not. I’ve made an appointment to see him at 10 o’clock today.
Hello, I’d like to know whether Steven is here or not. I’ve made an appointment to see him at 10 o’clock today.




E1
Are you Mr. Chen? I think you are looking for me as I’m the only Steven here. Please take a seat. How may I help you today?
27




M2
是这样，我的朋友建议我到这里来咨询一下律师，因为我在准备起诉我的房东，来捍卫自己的合理权益。因为我房东他过分了！ Look, my friend suggested that I come here and speak to some lawyers because I’m planning to sue my landlord and fight for my rights. He’s ridiculous!
Look, my friend suggested that I come here and speak to some lawyers because I’m planning to sue my landlord and fight for my rights. He’s ridiculous!




E2
It seems that you’ve got a problem or dispute with your landlord. But I can reassure you that you’re protected by the “Office for Fair Trading” as a tenant in New South Wales. Tell me, what’s the problem?
38




M3
两个星期之前，我跟我的房东说我想搬走。从那以后，他每天都带不同的人来我住的地方看房。我跟他表达过我的不满，但是他都不理不睬。
I told my landlord two weeks ago that I wanted to move out. Since then, he’s taken different people into the place for an open house every day.  I made a complaint to him but he just ignored me.




E3
You’re absolutely right in the sense that he can’t come whenever he likes. Landlords are only allowed to enter the premises at certain times for certain reasons and in most cases have to give you notice first.
37




M4
我也这样认为。当我问他怎么能这样做的时候，他只说他这是在保护他自己的权益。他想要尽快把这间房租出去。
That’s what I thought. When I asked him why he was doing that, he just told me that he’s protecting his rights by doing so.  He wants to rent the place out as soon as possible.




E4
If this matter cannot be resolved between you and your landlord, I’d suggest you take this to the Consumer, Trader and Tenancy Tribunal, which specialises in handling tenancy disputes.
30




M5
上法庭吗？那我需要请律师吗？会花很多钱吧？还有啊，我一点英语都不会讲，我跟法官沟通不了，可怎么办呢？
Going to court? Do I need to hire a lawyer? Does it cost a lot? On top of that,  I  don’t  speak  English  at  all,  what  if  I  can’t  communicate  with  the judge?




E5
You can represent yourself at the tribunal. The tribunal will provide interpreters to assist people whose first language is not English. As for the application fee, it’s $38 for tenancy disputes.
33




M6
那好，我明白了。我想问一下我可以去哪里拿一份申请表呢？今天真是麻烦你了。
OK,  I  understand.  I’m  just  wondering  where  I  can  get  a  copy  of  the application form. Thank you very much for today.




E6
I can give you an application form, just a second.
10
