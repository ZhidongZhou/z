母亲痴呆

相遇
30-05-2020
Hits
学习笔记 › CCL
CCL

Mr. Lu, a new Chinese immigrant in Australia, is talking to Joe, an ACAT (Aged Care Assessment Team) consultant, about placing his mother into an aged care centre.


        

        







ID
Original
Translated





E1
Good Morning, Mr. Lu. Thanks for coming in today. How can I help you?
14




M1
早上好，我最近在考虑帮我的母亲找一个合适的养老院，所以来咨询一下你。
Good morning, I’ve been considering finding my mother a good aged care centre. So I am here to make some enquiries.




E2
Certainly, in order to assist you better, could you give me some details on the applicant before we talk through the admission procedure?
23




M2
好的。我母亲得了老年痴呆症。一开始只是记忆力不好，比如说， 有一次她独自上街却忘记家在哪里了。还有就是，最近我觉得和她 沟通越来越困难了。
Sure. My mother has got senile dementia. At first, it was just that her memory wasn’t very good. For example, one day she went out on her own but later forgot where she lived. And I’ve found it’s getting more and more difficult to communicate with her recently.




E3
Well, I am sorry to hear that. It is quite normal for many of us to think we need to place our parents in residential care places when they have a condition that is severe or likely to worsen. // But it is not the only option. Often, people who are highly dependent often manage quite well at home with the help of a carer, family, friends or community services.
70




M3
我一开始也打算在家自己照顾她，但其实主要是我太太在照顾，我 工作比较忙。更糟糕的是，最近我母亲老是莫名其妙的和我太太起 争执。前几天洗澡的时候她摔断了胳膊，当时我就觉得要把她送老 人院了。
I originally planned to look after her at home. Well, actually my wife is the main carer as I am very busy at work. But what’s worse, my mother started to quarrel with my wife a lot for no reason. And when she broke her arm in the shower the other day, I realised it’s time to place her in aged care.




E4
I see. Well, there are currently a variety of aged care services offered in the community. I’m sure we can work together with you, and find the best arrangement for your mother. First, your mother will need to be assessed so we can decide what kind of service is best for her.
52




M4
恩，这没问题。但我想了解一下，这个评估是由谁来做的呢？评估 的流程又是怎样的呢？
That’s not a problem. But I’d like to know who will be the assessors and what the assessment procedure is like.




E5
The assessment is run by one or two members of the aged care assessment team or ACAT. They will visit your place and discuss with you whether community help will enable your mother to continue living at home or whether her needs would be better met through our aged care home.
51




M5
那你能告诉我，评估的时候通常会问哪些问题呢？这样我好提前准 备一些相关的书面材料给评估人员看。
Could you tell me what sorts of questions will be asked during the assessment? So that I can prepare some documents in advance to provide to the assessors.




E6
Questions asked at each assessment vary as they pertain to the individual, but they often include the types of daily living activities your mother needs help with, her general state of health and any other health issues she might have.
40




M6
我朋友说这边的养老院挺好的。但是我妈妈比较挑剔，所以希望您 帮忙找个好一点的，这样她就不会搬来搬去。
I heard from my friend that the aged care services here are quite nice. But my mother is a bit picky, so I hope you can help her find a good place to avoid having to move her again in the future.




E7
Well, Mr. Lu, I suggest that you fill out this application form first and we will see what results come from the assessment. Then we can make any further arrangements, Okay?
31




M7
好的，谢谢，我注意到外面的架子上有很多小册子，我可以去拿几 本回家看看吗？
Sure, thank you. I noticed there are many brochures on the shelf outside, can I take some to have a read at home？
