This dialogue takes place between a Chinese teacher who is visiting an Australian school and the headmaster of that school.

ID Original Translated
E1 感谢您能带我们参观校园。现在我想向您请教一些关于课程设置的问题。 Thanks for showing us around the campus. Now I’d like to ask you some things about your curriculum.
M1 It is my pleasure, Mr. He. When I visited schools in China I was also amazed by your way of teaching. So I’m more than willing to share some ideas regarding our curriculum with you. 35
E2 首先是主课。这个年纪的中国学生一般都会把语文看做一个非常枯燥的学科，而且特别怕写作文。而这里的学生则很享受语文的学习，这一点我很惊奇。 Firstly, regarding core subjects, in China, students of this age normally regard Chinese as a very dull subject and writing an essay is the last thing they want to do. By contrast, students here enjoy learning English very much, which is very surprising.
M2 We try to make English in Years 7 – 10 challenging but enjoyable. The aim is to improve students’ knowledge, skills and understanding of language by responding to and composing texts, including visual and multimedia texts. 35
E3 寓教于乐，我想这是我们应该借鉴的。另外，这里的学生学习数学可比中国学生轻松多了。而且，老师的教学似乎非常容易激发学生的兴趣。 Learning while having fun. I think that is something we can adopt. Besides, the maths study here is much easier than in China. Teachers seem very good at arousing students’ interest.
M3 We understand maths is an abstract subject which can easily frustrate students. That’s why we strive to create confidence and enjoyment in doing mathematical activities. Students develop an awareness of how maths can be used to solve problems in everyday life. 41
E4 我想中国的大多数学生还没有意识到，学校所学的知识和日常生活的相关性。这需要老师的正确引导。我注意到你们有各种各样的技术课，您能介绍一些这些课程吗？ I think most students in China haven’t realised the links between the knowledge they learn at school and their daily life. Teachers should guide them properly. I also noticed that you have various technology classes. Can you tell me a bit about them?
M4 The technology course gives students an appreciation of design and design processes. It lets them understand how humankind uses technology to develop creative and innovative solutions to real problems. For example, in food technology, students will learn how to prepare some basic food and create their own recipes. 48
E5 这在中国是不可想象的。因为我们并没有这样实用的生活化课程。那这些课程是选修还是必修？你们不担心学习这些课程会影响学生主课的学习？ It’s unimaginable in China, as we don’t have these kinds of practical courses that are so closely related to daily life. Then are these subjects elective or compulsory? Don’t you worry that studying these subjects will affect their results in core subjects?
M5 The Year 7-8 technology course is mandatory. After that the course is elective but students can choose, for example, food technology as one of their HSC courses. This means the grade for that course will be taken into account when students apply for university. 42
E6 这种体系很好，能让学生尽早认识自己的兴趣所在，从而能够在进入大学时明智地选择专业。但可惜，这在中国还行不通。 It’s a good system. It can help students find out what they are interested in as early as possible so that they can choose their majors wisely when entering university. But it is infeasible in China, which is quite unfortunate.
M6 Yes, different countries have different situations. China’s huge population does pose quite a challenge for workers in the education sector. 20
