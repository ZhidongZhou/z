新洲工作

相遇
06-06-2020
Hits
学习笔记 › NATTI › CCL
CCL 

        

        



Mr ZHONG is a newly arrived migrant, he has recently found a new job opportunity. Before accepting the new job, he goes to NSW Office of Industrial Relations to seek further information about working in NSW. Ms Jones, an IR officer meets Mr ZHONG at her office.








ID
Original
Translated





E1
我最近在找工作。有一个雇主要求我去面试，在我去之前，我想咨询您几个问题。
I’ve been looking for jobs recently. One employer asked me to attend an interview. But before I go, I would like to ask you a few questions.




M1
Congratulations！I will try to provide you with all the information that you might need. Before you start work you need to know the conditions you will be working and exactly what you will be expected to do in the role. Your employer should give you this information in a letter of offer.
54




E2
这封聘用书都具体都应该包含那些内容呢？我知道的有，我的工资，工作时间，我的职责等等。 但是我希望聘用书里面能包括公司的政策，因为它能反映用人单位的雇佣理念。我还希聘用书能列出一些对我有益处的职工培训项目。
What exactly should be included in the letter of offer? I only know there should be my salary, working hours and duties. But I hope it also includes company’s policies which normally reflect their employment philosophies, as well as some useful training programs that I may find beneficial.




M2
You are right. The letter of offer should include the employer’s name and contact details, your duties, salary, working hours, your employment conditions and whether you are to be employed as a casual, full-time or part-time employee.
37




E3
原来有这么多的细节信息呀。我本来想着特别简单， 就是我上班，雇主给钱。却不知道还有这么多不同的工作形式。
Oh so there is actually that much detailed information. I thought it would be just as simple as I work and get paid. But I didn’t know there are so many different types of jobs.




M3
Please remember to ask your employer what employment you will be working under and whether you are covered by a state or federal award.
24




E4
我明白了，每个细节都必须搞清楚。如果雇主给我书面聘用书，我应该收到后立刻签署相关的文件吗？
I see. I need to go through every detail. If the employer sends me an offer in writing, should I sign those documents as soon as they are provided?




M4
As part of the process of accepting a new job, you may be asked to sign a document agreeing to particular working conditions. This could be called a contract or an agreement. If this happens, make sure you read it very carefully first. Don’t feel pressured to sign straight away, especially if it doesn’t suit you.
56




E5
如果雇主没有给我书面的聘用书，我应该怎么办？我应该和他要吗？有的雇主嫌麻烦，不愿意出书面的聘用书。
What should I do if the employer is reluctant to present me with a written offer Should I ask for it? Some employers simply can’t be bothered to issue an offer letter.




M5
A verbal agreement is also a legal contract. One of the best things you can do is to note down everything the employer has told you about the job and your working conditions. Even though you have written it yourself, this can be used as evidence if there is any future dispute about your pay or conditions.
57




E6
我听说这边主要分两种工人，一种是合同制工人，一种是雇佣制工人。 而且雇员有全职工作， 半职工作， 还有临时工？如果雇主要求我做免费试工，我该怎么办？
I heard that there are two types of workers, under which I can either work as a contractor or employee and that I can work full time, part time or casually. What if they ask me to do an unpaid trial?




M6
Yes, you are right. It is important for all workers to know whether they are employees or contractors. Workers can be employed on a full-time, part-time or casual basis. It is against the law for prospective employers to make you do an unpaid work trial, even if it is just for a few hours.
54
