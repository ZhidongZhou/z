交规咨询

相遇
01-08-2020
Hits
学习笔记 › NATTI › CCL
CCL 

        

        




This is a dialogue between a new Mandarin-speaking migrant (M) and an officer of Victoria Roads (O).








ID
Original
Translated





E1
Here is your Victorian driver’s licence. Please be aware that to drive a car in Australia, you must have a driver’s licence with you and the vehicle you are driving must be registered with the government.
这是您的维多利亚州的驾照。请注意，在澳大利亚开车，您必须随身持有驾驶执照，而您所驾驶的车辆也必须跟政府登记。




M1
我知道，谢谢。这些规定跟中国几乎一样。我会谨慎驾驶的，但说实话，因为我当时只需要参加这里的路考，就可以把中国驾照转成澳洲驾照，所以在相关的法律法规方面，我并没有十足的把握。
I know, thank you. These regulations here are similar to those in China. I will be driving carefully. However, I don’t have full confidence in my knowledge on the relevant laws and regulations as I only needed to pass the road test here to have my Chinese licence converted to an Australian driver’s licence.




E2
There are quite a few things you should keep in mind, actually. Disobeying or breaking traffic laws can result in large fines, the loss of your driver’s licence or even imprisonment. Using a mobile phone while driving is against the law, unless you do so using a ‘hands free’ kit.
确实有一些需要注意的事项。不遵守或违反交通规则会导致大笔罚款、被吊销驾驶执照、甚至被监禁。驾驶时使用手机是违法的﹐除非您使用“免提”设备。




M2
这个我知道了，谢谢您的提醒。关于安全带呢？在中国，只有前排的人才必须系好安全带，对于坐在后排的人并没有硬性的规定。
Ok, I know, thank you for reminding me. What about seatbelts? In China, only people sitting in the front must wear seat belts. It’s not compulsory for back seat passengers.




E3
There are seatbelts or ‘restraints’ in all cars for adults and older children. The law states that everyone in your car must use a seatbelt or a proper child restraint, and if you are involved in a road accident you must report it to the police immediately.
所有的汽车都安装了给成人及大一些的孩子使用的安全带。法例规定，车上的每个人必须系上安全带或使用适当的儿童安全座椅。如果您发生交通意外，您必须马上报警。




M3
我只有一个 6 岁的儿子。我还去 Target 转过，儿童安全座椅还挺贵的呢。用不了几年，他就长大了，有点划不来。
I only have a 6-year-old son. I have been to Target looking for child restraints/capsules, but they are rather expensive. He will grow up soon, so he will only use it for a couple of years. It is not really cost effective.




E4
I see. In some areas it is possible to hire child safety restraints. To find out about the availability of this service in your area, you can contact the Kids Safety Centre Victoria, on 9244 6858.
我知道了。在某些地区是可以租用儿童安全座椅的。你可以拨打号码 9244 6858 给维多利亚州儿童安全中心，来查询你所在的地区是否提供该项服务。




M4
这可太好了。谢谢。我知道，有关超速驾驶及酒后驾驶的法例特别严格。但是法定的血液酒精含量每个州各有不同，并且要视乎所持有的驾驶执照级别。
Great. Thank you. I also know the laws are particularly strict regarding speed limits and driving after drinking alcohol. However, permitted blood alcohol levels vary, depending on the state or territory, and in accordance with the class of driver’s licence held.




E5
Yes, that’s correct. In Australia, you must be familiar with the Alcohol Guidelines. And it is illegal to drink alcohol while driving. A drink driving charge is a criminal charge and therefore if you are convicted of drink-driving, it will appear on your criminal record.
对，是这样的。在澳大利亚，你必须熟悉饮酒规定。而且，在驾驶时饮酒是违法的。酒驾是一项刑事指控，所以如果你因此被判有罪的话，会留下刑事犯罪记录。




M5
好的，我会回去好好研究一下这些规定，多加注意。不过我是生意人，几乎每个星期都有很多应酬。不能自己开车，就得想好别的后备计划。
Ok, I will study those policies closely and be very careful. But I am a business man, I have to attend different business banquets and parties almost every week. If I shouldn’t drive, I’ll have to figure out a plan-B.




E6
That’s a wise decision. I have to tell you, driving safely is not just for yourself, it impacts your family and the entire community as well.
这么想就对了。我必须告诉你，驾驶安全不仅仅是为了你一个人，这也关系到你的家庭和整个社会。




M6
我完全同意你说的。谢谢。
I can’t agree with you more. Thank you!
