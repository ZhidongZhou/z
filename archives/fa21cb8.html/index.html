辅导咨询

相遇
01-08-2020
Hits
学习笔记 › NATTI › CCL
CCL 

        

        




This is a dialogue between Mrs. Meng (M) and a counsellor (C).








ID
Original
Translated





E1
While preschool is for children between the ages of 2 and 6, and helps them to develop physically, emotionally and socially; Kindergarten is a kind of pre- schooling for children aged 4 to 6 to prepare them for primary school.
幼儿园招收年龄在 2 岁到 5 岁或 6 岁之间的儿童，让孩子们在身体、情感和社交方面得到发展。而学前班也是一种幼儿园服务，适合准 备升入小学的 4 至 6 岁的儿童。




M1
可是我女儿已经快 6 岁了。我可以让她先去上这种学前班么？要知道，她刚刚从国内大老远得过来，在这儿什么朋友也没有，除了弟弟，都没有人跟她一起玩儿。
But my daughter is nearly 6 years old. Can I send her to this kind of kindergarten first? You know, she just came to Australia all the way from China. She doesn’t have any friends here. She has no one to play with her, except for her younger brother.




E2
You can. But you must know in Australia, it is compulsory for children between 6 and 15 years old to go to school, and often continue until they are 17 or 18 years old, in preparation for university or other further education.
是的，可以。但你必须知道，在澳大利亚，所有 6 岁至 15 岁之间的孩子都必须去上学。直至他们 17 岁或 18 岁，就开始准备上大学或其他更高阶段的教育。




M2
我还知道，一般说来 12 岁或 13 岁以下的孩子上小学，年龄更大的孩子则上中学。你们还有政府公立学校和非政府学校，它们之间有什么区别？
I also know that in general children under 12 or 13 years old attend primary school and older children go to secondary school. And also there are government schools and non-government schools. What are the differences between them?




E3
Government schools provide free education. However, most schools ask for a voluntary contribution to enhance the school’s educational and sporting programs. Payment is up to parents. There are additional educational materials or services that parents can be expected to provide or pay the school to provide.
公立学校提供免费的教育，然而，大部份学校要求家长自愿资助学 校的教育和运动课程。付款与否由家长决定。额外的教学材料或服务，则需要家长自己提供，或是由家长付款给学校而由学校来提供。




M3
那基本上，家长就只需要给子女购买铅笔、钢笔、书本，以及有需要的话，还有校服等。对吗？那非政府学校呢？
Therefore, basically parents only need to provide their children with pencils, pens, textbooks, and school uniforms where appropriate. Is that correct? And what about non-government schools?




E4
Non-government schools charge fees, and they may have a religious affiliation or a particular educational philosophy. Parents who want to find out about private education should make an appointment with the relevant non- government educational authority or contact the selected school directly.
非政府学校要收费，而且它们可能会有宗教背景或者有特定的教育宗旨。想要了解有关私立教育情况的家长，应该与有关的私立教育管理 机构预约或者直接和所选的学校联络。




M4
可是我的孩子都是刚过来的，他们之前一直住在中国，一点儿英语也不 会说。我很担心他们上课的时候，什么也听不懂, 学习跟不上。
But both of my children have just arrived here. They were living in China before, so they can’t speak English at all. I am really worried that they may not understand anything or not be able to keep up.




E5
Newly arrived children who don’t speak English should enrol directly in English Language Schools (ELS) or English Language Centres (ELC) which provide intensive English programs designed for children. These are located in metropolitan areas and in some larger country centres.
刚抵达不会说英语的孩子﹐可报读 ELS 英语学校及 ELC 英语中心 ﹐他们提供特别为孩子而设的强化英语课程。他们在市区内及一些较大的乡镇中心开设。




M5
我们就住在市中心，这应该不难找。 那我怎么给他们报名呢？报名时要带哪些材料呢？
We live in the city, so it should be easy to find one. What should I do for enrolment? What kinds of documents should I bring with me?




E6
To enrol your child in a school, contact the school by telephone or in person. You will need to take your visa or entry to Australia documents, proof of your child’s date of birth, and any papers including school reports relating to their previous education.
若要给您的孩子报名上学，请打电话或亲自去学校联系。您将需要 带上您的签证或来澳大利亚的入境文件、您的孩子的出生日期的证明、以及和他们以前的教育有关的任何文件，包括成绩单。




M6
好的，我明白了。谢谢您的宝贵时间。
Alright, I see. Thank you for your time!
